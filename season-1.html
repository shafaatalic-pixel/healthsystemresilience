§ 01 · The central question

Can a health system be understood as economic infrastructure?

Season 1 answers yes — and shows what follows for households, institutions, and public trust when that infrastructure holds or fails.

Across two countries and nine pieces, one argument: the resilience of a health system is not a humanitarian afterthought but a form of economic protection. When illness, debt, disaster, or workforce collapse strike, resilient systems protect livelihoods; fragile ones transmit the shock into households and budgets.

§ 03 · Impact & methodology

Engagement as advocacy intelligence.

The campaign reached 57,274 impressions with no paid promotion — 5.7× its 10,000-impression success criterion. Four replicated findings on the homepage explain how the argument moved.

What was measured: impressions, audience geography and composition, and platform routing, compiled from platform-native analytics (Meta, LinkedIn, Instagram) and reconciled at >94% against full-campaign locks; Instagram adjusted for cross-post referral traffic.

DATES — Figures reflect the 8-week campaign window; each article also carries its original publication date. RIGHTS — The Special Report is cleared for full reproduction; the six outlet chapters require republication permission before full-text use and currently link to the original outlets.
